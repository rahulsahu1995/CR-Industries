about:-The Soudal Groups Is Building The Future
The Soudal Group is the largest independent European manufacturer of sealants, adhesives and polyurethane foams for professional and private users. This Belgian family business, based in Turnhout and founded by Vic Swerts in 1966, has developed into an international player and expert in chemical construction specialities.

The group has 19 production branches on 5 continents, sales offices in 44 different countries and employs around 3,100 people worldwide. A long-term vision for innovation – with substantial investment in R&D – and adaptations to local market requirements have resulted in the success of the Soudal Group.

Soudal HQ

Innovation
The research in and development of new products, product technologies and production methods is in our genes. A team of highly qualified researchers, engineers and technicians is continuously working on the development of new products and applications adapted to the specific requirements of customers in various countries. This has resulted in a number of pioneering innovative products, which have helped to strengthen the market position of Soudal. Innovation is, therefore, the driver for organic growth at the Soudal Group.
Facts & Figures
A consolidated turnover of €835 million in 2018; 350 million sealant and polyurethane foam units produced in 19 production branches; around 3,100 employees worldwide; almost €59 million in investment in 2018: those are the impressive facts and figures of the Soudal Group.

Our Activities
The Soudal Group manufactures products for its own brands and private labels covering 7 groups: silicones and other sealants, polyurethane foams, adhesives, hybrid polymers, technical aerosols and chemical building products. With its own brands the Soudal Group is focusing on 3 market segments: the construction, industrial and retail sectors.
Sustainability
The Soudal Group is leading the way in the development of products for sustainable construction, which are safe for people and the environment. As a result of the most modern production technology, energy, water usage, and residual waste is limited. It is obvious that the welfare of employees is another priority.



Sponsoring
From 2023 onwards, the Soudal-Quick Step team is representing Soudal at all large international cycling competitions and provides publicity in 190 countries where cycling makes it into the media (especially the Tour de France). The Soudal Quick-Step investment increases our brand awareness and company image. The basic values propagated by Soudal – hard work, perseverance and being better than your competitors – can also be found in cycling, leading to a positive image transfer to the Soudal brand. And most importantly, Soudal Quick-Step is a huge platform for building Soudal marketing actions.

Besides of Soudal Quick-Step, Soudal also sponsors KVC Westerlo (football), Soudal Classics (cyclocross) and Soudal Open (Golf – Belgian Open).













 company name :- CUMI
about :- Carborundum Universal Limited (CUMI), established as a tripartite joint venture in 1954, is a leading materials sciences engineering solutions provider. CUMI’s consolidated revenue is ₹ 4834 crores and PAT of ₹ 293 crores for the financial year 2024-25. CUMI, part of the 125-year-old Murugappa Group, is listed on the NSE and BSE. CUMI is a Mines to Market Company whose integrated operations include mining, power generation, fusion, manufacturing, marketing and distribution. CUMI has over 10,000 employees worldwide who collaborate, innovate and develop high-quality material solutions and world-class services in Abrasives, Electrominerals, Ceramics, and Refractories, serving customers in diverse industries including engineering, fabrication, auto and auto components, infrastructure, steel, glass, power generation and distribution, mining and aerospace. CUMI has a wide geographical presence spanning six continents and exports products to over 60 countries.

Murugappa Ajax Furniture Company in established in 1938 and CUMI started its voyage in 1954 as a tripartite venture between Carborundum of the USA, Universal Grinding Wheel, UK and the Murugappa Group, India.

1965 – Bauxite Calcination Plant at Okha


The Electro Minerals Division of CUMI, also known as CUMI Minerals, started as a supportive division to its Captive units – Abrasives & Super Refractories. Its supreme manufacturing facilities are strategically located in the Indian states of Kerala and Gujrat, leveraging maximum advantages of raw material availability, power costs, closeness to customers, logistic facilities and most importantly, availability of a large well trained work force.

The journey started in the year 1964, when CUMI Minerals commissioning its Brown Fused Alumina Plant at Kalamassery, Cochin. The very next year, CUMI spread its productive presence across India with a different perspective and advance technology added with maximum facilities. In The year 1965 CUMI started The Bauxite Mines and Calcination plant in Okha, Gujarat. This secured the Raw Material needs for Brown Fused Alumina manufacturing and even today continues to suffice the Bauxite requirements of CUMI.




1984 – Silicon carbide plant, Koratty, India


1988 – Silicon carbide Micro grit plant, Koratty, India


CUMI Minerals set forth its journey into non-oxide minerals. The Kerala Village Koratty at Thrissur witnessed the flourishing of a novel production unit of Silicon Carbide in 1984. Quick to realize the vast applications of SiC micro grits CUMI commissioned The SiC Micro Grit Plant at Koratty in 1988.



1992 – Fused Alumina, Plant 2, Edappally


1994 – Mini-Hydel Plant at Maniyar


Knowledge in fusion helped the expansion into White Fused Alumina and Fused Mullite. In 1992, CUMI started White Fused Alumina Plant with a Tilt Furnace at Kalamassery.

Apart from Bauxite, power is a key input in the manufacturing of CUMI Minerals. To secure this, CUMI Started the Hydro Electric Plant in Kerala at Maniyar near Pathanamthitta in the year 1994. This was another landmark which was a great support to its energy intensive units and today caters to more than 30% of the power requirements of the captive power requirements.



2004- Modern Micro grit plant, Koratty, India


2006 – AzureS (Sol-Gel) plant


Micro Grit Plant in Koratty was modernized in 2004 to enhance quality controls and product deliverables. Our most promising plant, the AzureS (Sol-Gel) Plant was commissioned in 2006 at Kalamassery. This plant today competes at a global level with leading players as a preferred product for both Bonded & Coated applications.




2007 – VAW, Russia


Global Expansion of EMD came about with the acquisition of the Russian Unit, Volzhsky Abrasive Works in 2007. VAW is the 2nd largest manufacturer of SiC in the world and this put CUMI in the global arena of mineral players. CUMI then made its second global acquisition into the strategic materials of Zirconia with the acquisition of Foskor, Zirconia in South Africa in 2008. With this, CUMI added the Calcia Stabilised Zirconia & Monoclinic Zirconia products to widen its offering to the refractory market. 2008 was one another remarkable year which witnessed the initiation of the Treatments Plant at Kalamassery. CUMI Minerals today offers a wide variety of treated grains including – heat treated, blue fired, Red oxide or CC, High Temperature Ceramic Coating, Silane & also Chemical Coatings.
